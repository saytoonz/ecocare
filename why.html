ECOCARE WASTE INITIATIVES (EWI)

EcoCare(Ghana)

EcoCare Waste Indicatives is a low tech recycling company that is that is aimed at solving Ghana's waste handling challenge with an approach differently from the conventional waste management approach - by imploring inclusively social, environmental and economic impact models. Thus EcoCare Waste Initiative is a social enterprise.
Therefore, EcoCare Waste Initiatives is a social enterprise established in Ghana to address the challenges of plastic pollution, rural and per-urban waste management, unemployment, deforestation and climate change. EcoCare plans to achieve these by utilizing waste plastic as a resource to manufacture aesthetic, durable and ecologically friendly fencing posts and landscaping artefacts and packages. The mission of EcoCare is to employ the talents of a team of environmentally conscious Ghanaians - to save our forests and ensure environmental health through promotion of our low-tech recycling innovations.


MISSION AND VISIONS

Our Vision

Our vision is "To transform Ghana into a waste free state"

Our Mission

Our mission is "To challenge Ghanaians wasteful paradigm and throw-away culture by giving value to waste while advocating against waste generation."

Our goal in the short to medium term is to create a sustainable solution to the growing plastic and organic waste menace, alternatives to landfills and chemical fertilizer while providing decent job opportunities in the environmental and sanitation sectors in our communities. Our goal in the long term will be to scale up into a big social enterprise that will produce and supply Ghanaians with green energy (like solar, biogas and other biofuels) and shift Ghana's dependence on timber for furniture onto recycled plastic. Key strategies we use are: to Design low-tech waste recycling ideas; Inspire people with our ideas, to challenge the wasteful paradigm; and to Promote recycled products "Don't Waste Your Waste," is our commitment motto to environmental excellence integrated with a business approach to sustainability.


Objectives

a. To develop low-tech reuse and recycling strategies and products from all recyclable plastic, paper and organic waste

b. To educate the world to challenge the wasteful paradigm

c. To earn a sustainable economic, social and ecological value in the Ghanaian waste management sector

d. To reduce the world's waste menace (especially plastic and paper) drastically.

PROBLEM STATEMENTS

1. The throw-away culture of Ghanaians lead to indiscriminate disposal of municipal and industrial waste - creating huge challenge in waste collection for Zoomlion (Ghana's waste collection giant); and sanitation problems for the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and Town councils

2. Most of the rural communities and suburbs of Goaso (the capital) in the Asunafo North Municipality (where we are based) are not yet covered by the Zoomlion-Municipal Waste Management concession.

3. Increased pressure on landfills - where more arable lands are rather used as dump sites by the Zoomlion waste management activities

4. Climate change - due to increased Carbon dioxide (CO2) emission from burning of wastes, and

5. Soil depletion and pollution of groundwater and rivers - from hazardous chemicals spillage and indiscriminate application of chemical fertilizers.


OUR PHILOSOPY

The philosophy of EcoCare comes from the motivation of the founder:

"I discovered after many years of being a 'pack rat' that: many product packages (especially those of paper and plastic) that we use in our homes, offices and industries, usually have some after-use values that we tend to neglect. And I have always wondered what could be done to harness this neglected resource. Plastic bottles for instance are designed to carry liquids of all sort. Billions are spent on their design and product marketing. However, once a plastic drink bottle is empty it is perceived by the average Ghanaian as redundant and thrown away. So I sort to challenge this wasteful paradigm. My project philosophy and lifestyle philosophy combine to sum up my motivation for this social enterprise: "Every waste has a potential, if we challenge our wasteful paradigm we would find them." - Mr. Jackson Nyarko

SUSTAINABILITY OF ECOCARE

We hope to use the following Social, Environmental and Economic (TBL/PPP) approaches to make our project sustainable:

1. In-depth Research and development activities at our "EcoCare Recycling Valley"

2. Run a website and an information desk to educate the world on eco-friendly waste management while promoting recycled products

3. Radio shows and community campaigns on how to manage waste the eco-friendly way, while marketing our recycled products and ideas

4. Waste collection business to make money to pay for staff and volunteers allowances and other expenses

5. Production and sales of recycled products to make economic profits

6. Organize training workshops and exhibition conferences aimed at developing creative recycling and manufacturing ideas in young people, while generating funds (IGF).

7. Scaling up in future to include production of green energy like biogas; and manufacturing of plastic fence posts
